Types Of ChatBot

There are different types of chatbots, and they can be classified based on various criteria such as functionality, technology, and design.

AI-powered chatbots

These chatbots use machine learning algorithms to learn from past interactions and improve their responses over time. They can understand and interpret natural language input and provide more personalized and accurate responses.

Rule-based chatbots

These chatbots are programmed to follow a set of predefined rules and provide predefined responses based on specific keywords or phrases. They are typically less complex and less expensive to develop but are limited in their ability to handle complex queries.

Social media chatbots

These chatbots are integrated with social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram, and allow users to interact with businesses and brands through messaging.

Conversational chatbots

These chatbots are designed to simulate human conversation and engage users in a natural and interactive way. They use a combination of NLP, AI, and machine learning to understand user intent, provide relevant information, and respond in a conversational tone.

Task-oriented chatbots

These chatbots are designed to perform specific tasks such as booking a flight, ordering food, or scheduling an appointment. They use a conversational interface to guide the user through the task and provide relevant information.

Voice chatbots

These chatbots use voice recognition technology to enable users to interact with them through voice commands. They are commonly used in voice assistants such as Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant, and Apple Siri.
